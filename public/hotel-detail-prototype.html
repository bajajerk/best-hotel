About Komaneka at Bisma

Komaneka at Bisma sits above the Campuhan Ridge, where Ubud's mist rises each morning before the valley clears. It's a place that rewards staying in as much as going out — the kind of hotel where breakfast stretches to eleven and no one minds.

The pool runs the length of the garden, level with the treetops. Rooms open onto the ridge; bathrooms onto small stone courtyards you'll forget are private. A short walk takes you to the monkey forest or the warungs along Bisma; a shorter one returns you to a deep chair on the veranda.

Gede, who has run the front desk for eleven years, keeps a list of things worth doing in the valley — the walks, the temple mornings, the one rice farmer who still makes red-rice porridge on a wood fire. Ask for it. It is better than the one in the folder.
